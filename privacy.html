2) Information We Collect

  • Account Data: name, email, password (hashed), optional phone, country/city (if provided).
  • Transactional Data: orders, invoices, license keys, payout details (for Sellers). Payment card data is handled by our payment processor (e.g., Stripe); we do not store full card numbers.

  • Usage & Device Data: IP address, device/browser type, pages viewed, timestamps, referrer/UTM, approximate geolocation (from IP), error logs.
  • Support Communications: emails, tickets, attachments, chat transcripts.
  • Cookies & Similar Tech: see our Cookie Notice for details and choices.

3) How We Use Information

  • Provide the Services: account creation, authentication, order processing, license delivery, payouts.
  • Improve & secure: troubleshoot, debug, prevent fraud/abuse, monitor uptime and performance.
  • Communications: transactional emails (receipts, alerts), service notices, and—with your consent or where permitted—product updates.
  • Compliance: tax, accounting, KYC/KYB, regulatory requests, enforcing our Terms and policies.
  • Analytics: aggregated metrics to understand usage patterns and make product decisions.

4) Legal Bases (EEA/UK)

Where GDPR/UK GDPR applies, we process data under these bases:

  • Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)): to provide the Services you request (orders, payouts, support).
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): to secure Services, prevent abuse, improve features, and perform limited analytics.
  • Legal obligations (Art. 6(1)(c)): tax, accounting, KYC/KYB, fraud prevention laws.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): where required (e.g., certain cookies/marketing). You can withdraw consent at any time.

5) Sharing & Disclosures

  • Processors: trusted vendors who help us run the Services (e.g., cloud hosting/CDN, payment processing, email delivery, analytics, error monitoring). They process data under contracts and only on our instructions.

  • Legal & Safety: to comply with laws, court orders, tax and regulatory obligations, or to protect rights, safety, and integrity of RGZTEC and users.
  • Business Transfers: in a merger, acquisition, or asset sale, your data may be transferred consistent with this Policy.

6) International Transfers

We may process data in the United States and other countries. Where required, we use appropriate safeguards (e.g., EU Standard Contractual Clauses) for transfers from the EEA/UK. You may contact us for a copy of relevant transfer mechanisms.

7) Retention

We retain personal data for as long as necessary to provide the Services, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. Typical examples:

  • Account and order records: for the life of the account and as required by tax/accounting laws (often 5–10 years).
  • Support tickets/logs: typically 12–36 months, unless needed longer for security/audit.
  • If you request deletion, we will anonymize or delete data unless we must keep it to meet legal obligations.

8) Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights over your personal data. These can include:

  • Access to the data we hold about you; Rectification of inaccurate data.
  • Erasure (deletion) and Restriction of processing in certain cases.
  • Portability of data you provided to us, in a machine-readable format.
  • Objection to processing based on legitimate interests and to direct marketing.
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.

To exercise rights, email privacy@rgztec.com. We may verify your identity and respond within statutory timeframes. If you are in the EEA/UK, you can also lodge a complaint with your data protection authority.
